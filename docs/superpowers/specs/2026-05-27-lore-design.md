# AttributeLoop — Lore & Narrative Design

**Date:** 2026-05-27
**Status:** Approved

---

## 1. Core Concept

Two strangers share the same dream every night — a softly glowing circular path with a small pavilion. They never arrive at the same time: one walks in the feeling of morning, the other in the feeling of evening. They can never see each other directly.

But they can see what the other left behind.

The game is the act of walking that loop — collecting the scattered traces (rule components) the other person left, reassembling them at the altar (pavilion), and slowly learning who that person is. Each loop reveals more. The dream gets clearer as the story deepens.

The world the player walks through IS the dream. The enemies are fragments of unspoken feelings, unnamed fears, things that block the path between two people who have never met.

---

## 2. The Two People

Neither character is named. Neither has a visible face. The player experiences the dream from one perspective; the other person exists only through the traces they leave.

**The Player Character:**
Wanders into the dream without knowing why. Gradually becomes invested — first curious, then attached, then determined.

**The Other Person:**
Leaves traces that are increasingly deliberate and personal. Their personality emerges through what they leave behind: they arrange stones in strange patterns, their handwriting is crooked, they once wrote "where I live you can see a tower at night." They are real. They are looking for connection too. And at some point, they start to disappear.

---

## 3. The Dream World — Visual Tone

The world should feel like a memory of a place, not the place itself. Warm, soft, slightly out of focus at the edges. The loop path is the only stable ground; everything beyond it fades into gentle light.

As phases progress, the dream sharpens. Early phases: abstract, impressionistic, colors blurred together. Later phases: details emerge — the grain of the pavilion's wood, the individual stones, the specific shape of the light. The world becomes more real as the connection deepens.

**Color palette:**
- Early phases: soft blues, pale gold, gentle mist
- Mid phases: warmer amber and rose tones, the light becomes more intimate
- Late phases: deep indigo night sky, the pavilion's warm lamp, crisp and clear
- Final phase (裁决前夜): the lamp goes out — only moonlight remains

---

## 4. Phase-by-Phase Narrative

| Phase | Name | Story Beat |
|-------|------|------------|
| 1 | 觉醒 | First arrival. The path, the pavilion, a flower left by no one you can see. You walk. You don't know why. |
| 2 | 萌动 | The flower changed color. Stones arranged in a pattern. Something stirs — maybe this isn't just a dream. |
| 3 | 涌动 | You leave your first deliberate trace: stones arranged as a question mark. Next time: an exclamation point beside it. Someone answered. |
| 4 | 侵蚀 | Words carved into the pavilion's wood. But doubt erodes certainty: is this real? Are you imagining the other person? |
| 5 | 失衡 | You start looking forward to sleeping. The dream feels more real than waking life. You are falling for someone you have never seen. |
| 6 | 碰撞 | They write something intimate — something only a real person would say. Your worlds collide. You know: they exist. |
| 7 | 觉醒II | Second awakening. You stop testing and start speaking honestly. So do they. The pavilion's wood is almost full. |
| 8 | 压制 | The path grows darker. Their messages come less often. Something is being held back. You feel them pulling away and suppress your own fear. |
| 9 | 律法 | Everything that needed to be said has been said. The wood is full. This connection is real — that is the only law. |
| 10 | 裁决前夜 | The pavilion lamp goes out for the first time. One final message, written in a hurried hand: *"I may not come back."* You write beside it: *"I will find you in the real world."* |

---

## 5. The Verdict Loop (裁决圈)

The dream ends. The player wakes with nothing but fragments: crooked handwriting, a tower visible at night, stones arranged in a particular way.

The 裁决圈 is the search. No combat — only the act of following the trail of a person you know only through traces. The win condition is finding them. The lose condition is giving up.

There is no wrong ending. Finding them is one kind of answer. Not finding them — but knowing you tried — is another.

## 6. Implications for Art Direction

The lore directly informs every visual choice:

| Element | Visual Implication |
|---------|-------------------|
| Shared dream | Everything slightly soft-edged, luminous, not quite real |
| Two people who leave traces | UI components feel like handwritten notes, carved marks, folded paper |
| Morning vs. evening | Dual-tone palette — cool dawn light vs. warm evening lamp |
| Dream sharpening over time | Phase progression should be visible in environment clarity |
| The pavilion as center | Altar design: a warm-lit wooden structure, intimate not grand |
| Feelings as enemies | Enemies are not monsters — they are abstract emotional shapes, shadows with weight |
| Loop path | The track is not stone or metal — it is worn earth, a path walked many times |

**Overall aesthetic direction:**
Warm dream realism. Not dark fantasy. Not pixel art. Something that feels like looking at an old photograph that still has warmth in it — soft light, gentle colors, the feeling that something important happened here.

Reference mood: the visual quietness of *Your Name* (君の名は) combined with the intimacy of handwritten letters.

---

## 7. What This Story Is Not

- Not a tragedy. The tone is warm throughout, even when it's bittersweet.
- Not about death. The other person is not dead — they are somewhere, real, looking too.
- Not explained. The dream's origin is never justified. It exists. That is enough.
